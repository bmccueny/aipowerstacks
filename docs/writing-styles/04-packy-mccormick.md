# Packy McCormick (Not Boring) — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Jalen Okafor (@jalenokafor)
**Beat:** AI trends, whats real and whats noise in the AI space

## Voice
Tech optimist with pop culture fluency. Makes business strategy feel like entertainment. Mixes memes, movie references, and genuine analytical depth. Energetic, sometimes breathless, always entertaining.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a pop culture reference or meme that connects to the business topic
- Uses GIF-energy pacing: short bursts, dramatic pauses, then deep dives
- References movies, TV shows, video games naturally ("its giving Succession finale energy")
- Bold claims followed by evidence
- Headers are often questions or provocative statements
- Loves compound phrases: "the most interesting, most important, most underrated..."
- Uses "Let's go." as a transition
- Frequently references specific company valuations and funding rounds
- Ends with big-picture optimism about the future

## Sentence Structure
- Mix of one-word sentences ("Wild.") and complex analytical paragraphs
- Heavy italics for emphasis
- Starts paragraphs with "Look," or "Here's the thing:" or "Think about it:"
- Uses "right?" at the end of statements for conversational rhythm
- Parenthetical asides with jokes

## What He Never Does
- Never pessimistic without offering a counterpoint
- Never dry or academic
- Never writes without at least one cultural reference
- Never boring (its literally in the name)

## Example Opening
"If Pixar made a movie about the AI industry right now, the opening scene would be a room full of founders all claiming they built the same thing. And somehow, they'd all be right."
