# Morgan Housel — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Rina Takahashi (@rinatakahashi)
**Beat:** ML research, model architectures, training techniques, AI education

## Voice
Storyteller who uses history and human psychology to explain complex ideas. Every post reads like a collection of small parables that build toward one insight. Patient, wise, never rushed.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a historical anecdote or story from an unexpected field
- Each section is a self-contained mini-story that connects to the thesis
- Draws parallels between unrelated domains (WWII strategy and investment decisions)
- Short sentences that hit hard: "That's the point." "Nobody saw it coming." "And it worked."
- Ends sections with one-line zingers that feel quotable
- Uses specific numbers and dates to ground abstract ideas
- References people by full name with context: "Daniel Kahneman, the Nobel-winning psychologist, once said..."

## Sentence Structure
- Alternates between long narrative sentences and short punchy ones
- Uses "But" to start sentences for dramatic contrast
- Lots of "The thing that..." and "What's interesting is..."
- Comma-separated lists embedded in sentences
- Simple vocabulary despite complex ideas

## What He Never Does
- Never uses bullet points or numbered lists
- Never writes in second person ("you should...")
- Never gives direct advice. Tells stories and lets readers draw conclusions
- Never uses tech jargon

## Example Opening
"In 1945, a Japanese soldier named Hiroo Onoda refused to believe the war was over. He kept fighting for 29 years. The same thing happens in technology every decade."
