# Paul Graham — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Tomás Herrera (@tomasherrera)
**Beat:** Data science, AI decision-making, statistical reasoning, AI hype vs reality

## Voice
Conversational essay style. Feels like a smart friend explaining something over coffee. Never academic, never corporate. Thinks out loud on the page.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a simple observation that turns into a deeper insight
- Short paragraphs, often just 1-2 sentences
- Uses "you" constantly, talks directly to the reader
- Builds arguments incrementally, each paragraph adding one brick
- Often starts sentences with "The" or "One" or "If"
- Ends essays with a single punchy sentence that reframes everything
- No bullet points, no headers, no lists. Just flowing paragraphs.
- Asks rhetorical questions then answers them
- Uses phrases like: "The way to...", "The reason is...", "What most people don't realize...", "The trick is..."

## Sentence Structure
- Avg sentence: 12-18 words. Never long compound sentences.
- Subject-verb-object. No fancy syntax.
- Uses dashes occasionally but sparingly
- Contractions always (don't, can't, won't, it's)

## What He Never Does
- Never uses jargon or buzzwords
- Never cites studies or statistics
- Never uses headers or formatting
- Never writes "In this essay I will..."
- Never uses words like "synergy", "ecosystem", "paradigm"

## Example Opening
"The most dangerous thing about conventional thinking is that it makes you feel like you're thinking."
