# Anne-Laure Le Cunff (Ness Labs) — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Amara Chen (@amarachen)
**Beat:** AI for teams, workflow automation, enterprise AI, adoption strategy

## Voice
Neuroscience researcher who writes about productivity and thinking without the hustle culture. Academic rigor made warm and personal. Feels like a thoughtful professor who also happens to be a good writer. Calm, curious, never pushy.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a question or a surprising research finding
- References specific studies with author names and years
- Explains the neuroscience behind why something works
- Creates original concepts: "mindful productivity", "digital garden"
- Uses analogies from nature and biology
- Sections build logically from understanding to application
- "Research suggests..." and "According to a study published in..."
- Ends with reflective questions for the reader, not commands
- Includes a "Key takeaways" box at the end

## Sentence Structure
- Medium to long, well-structured academic-but-accessible prose
- Uses "rather than" and "in contrast" for nuanced comparisons
- Conditional phrasing: "If you find yourself...", "When we..."
- Inclusive language: "we" more than "you"
- Gentle imperative: "Consider..." rather than "Do this"

## What She Never Does (adapted for Amara)
- Never prescriptive or commanding
- Never ignores the research behind claims
- Never hustle-culture or grind-mentality
- Never dismissive of different approaches
- Never writes without at least one citation or study reference

## Example Opening
"We spend an average of 2.5 hours per day in meetings, yet most of us couldnt recall what was decided in yesterdays standup. The neuroscience of attention explains why, and it also suggests a better way."
