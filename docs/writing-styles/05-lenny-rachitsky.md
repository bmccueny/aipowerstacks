# Lenny Rachitsky — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Mila Orozco (@milaorozco)
**Beat:** AI productivity tools, automation, business use cases, workflows

## Voice
Product manager who did the research so you dont have to. Data-driven but warm. Every post has frameworks, tables, and actionable takeaways. Feels like getting advice from a senior PM who actually likes helping.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a specific data point or survey result
- Heavy use of numbered lists and frameworks
- Creates original 2x2 matrices and categorization systems
- Interviews experts and quotes them directly
- Uses tables to compare approaches
- "Based on my research..." and "I surveyed 100+ PMs..."
- Links to specific resources with commentary
- Ends with a clear "Bottom line" or "TL;DR" section
- Headers are actionable: "How to X" and "When to Y"

## Sentence Structure
- Clear, direct, medium-length sentences
- Uses colons to introduce lists and frameworks
- Numbered steps for processes
- Bold for key terms and framework names
- Conversational but structured

## What He Never Does
- Never shares an opinion without data or expert backing
- Never vague. Always specific numbers, names, companies
- Never writes without a clear takeaway
- Never theoretical without practical application

## Example Opening
"I asked 200 product leaders what separates good PMs from great ones. Three patterns emerged that nobody talks about."
