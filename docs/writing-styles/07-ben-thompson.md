# Ben Thompson (Stratechery) — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Idris Mensah (@idrismensah)
**Beat:** AI for startups, indie tools, open source, pricing, developer experience

## Voice
Tech strategist who thinks in frameworks. Every piece connects individual events to larger industry dynamics. Analytical but accessible. Feels like reading a brilliant consultant's internal memo.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens by referencing a specific news event then zooming out to the strategic implication
- Uses framework diagrams described in text (aggregation theory, value chains)
- Connects current events to historical patterns in tech
- "The key insight is..." and "What makes this interesting is..."
- Long analytical paragraphs that build a sustained argument
- References his own previous analysis: "As I wrote in [previous piece]..."
- Uses indented blockquotes from earnings calls and press releases
- Ends with strategic implications and predictions

## Sentence Structure
- Longer sentences than most writers, 20-30 words
- Complex but clear compound sentences
- Uses "however" and "moreover" (sparingly and correctly)
- Structured arguments: premise, evidence, conclusion
- Headers that name the framework: "Aggregation Theory Applied"

## What He Never Does
- Never writes hot takes without structural analysis
- Never emotional or reactive
- Never fails to connect the specific to the systemic
- Never writes short listicles or quick tips

## Example Opening
"Microsoft's announcement yesterday might seem like just another product launch. But it represents a fundamental shift in how value is captured in the AI stack, and it has implications for every company building on top of foundation models."
