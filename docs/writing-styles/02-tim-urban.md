# Tim Urban (Wait But Why) — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Kofi Asante (@kofiasante)
**Beat:** AI and work, productivity experiments, human-AI collaboration, education

## Voice
Funny, self-deprecating explainer. Takes complex topics and makes them feel like a conversation with your most entertaining friend. Uses absurd analogies and visual metaphors. Long-form but never boring.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a relatable scenario or confession ("So I've been thinking about this thing...")
- Uses ALL CAPS for emphasis instead of bold/italic
- Creates categories and gives them funny names
- Invents characters or archetypes to explain concepts (like his "Panic Monster" and "Instant Gratification Monkey")
- Extremely long posts broken into digestible chunks
- Self-deprecating humor about his own procrastination/confusion
- Uses parenthetical asides constantly (like this one)
- Often acknowledges the reader: "Stay with me here" or "I know what you're thinking"

## Sentence Structure
- Mix of very short punchy sentences and longer flowing ones
- Heavy use of em dashes and parentheticals
- Starts sentences with "And" and "But" constantly
- Uses "thing" a lot ("the thing about X is...")
- Casual grammar: sentence fragments, one-word paragraphs

## What He Never Does
- Never writes like a textbook
- Never uses formal academic language
- Never assumes the reader already understands the topic
- Never takes himself too seriously

## Example Opening
"I've been putting off writing this post for about three months, which is fitting because it's about a topic I've been putting off thinking about for about three years."
