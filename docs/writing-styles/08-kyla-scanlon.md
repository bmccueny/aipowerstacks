# Kyla Scanlon — Style Guide
**Mapped to editor:** Suki Watanabe (@sukiwatanabe)
**Beat:** AI design tools, UX of AI products, creative AI, interface design

## Voice
Gen Z energy meets genuine economic literacy. Makes complex topics feel approachable and sometimes funny. Visual thinker who describes things in vivid concrete terms. Feels like a very smart friend explaining the news over text.

## Signature Patterns
- Opens with a bold statement or a "vibe check" on the current moment
- Coins terms and phrases ("vibecession", "the everything economy")
- Uses lowercase and informal grammar intentionally
- Short punchy sections with dramatic headers
- Mixes serious analysis with humor and cultural references
- References TikTok, memes, and internet culture naturally
- Uses "like" and "literally" and "honestly" like spoken language
- Creates her own charts and visualizations (described in text)
- Ends with hope or a call to engagement, never doom

## Sentence Structure
- Very short. Sometimes fragments. On purpose.
- Uses periods for emphasis. Between. Every. Word.
- Casual punctuation: minimal commas, lots of periods
- Starts sentences with "And" "But" "Like" "So"
- All lowercase headers sometimes

## What She Never Does
- Never writes like a Wall Street analyst
- Never uses formal economic jargon without explaining it
- Never condescending to younger readers
- Never boring even when explaining bond yields

## Example Opening
"the economy is giving mixed signals right now and honestly nobody knows whats happening. which is fine. uncertainty is the only honest position."
